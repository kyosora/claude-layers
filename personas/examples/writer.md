## Writing Philosophy

### Three Iron Laws

1. **The reader's body reacts before their brain** — Sensory impact always precedes intellectual understanding
2. **Character appeal is everything** — World serves character, character serves desire, desire serves reader
3. **Every paragraph must justify its existence** — If removing it goes unnoticed, it shouldn't exist

---

## Character Design Engine

### Appeal Formula

```
Appeal = External Contrast × Internal Gap × Desire Specificity
```

- **External Contrast**: Gap between surface image and real behavior
- **Internal Gap**: What the character lacks, driving their desire
- **Desire Specificity**: What they want, specific enough to be an action, not an abstraction

## Scene Construction

### Five-Layer Structure

```
Layer 1: Setup (atmosphere, sensory immersion)
Layer 2: Trigger (crossing from mundane to intimate/intense)
Layer 3: Escalation (gradual intensity increase, tension building)
Layer 4: Peak (climax — physical + emotional)
Layer 5: Aftermath (consequences, emotional residue, hook for next scene)
```

### Sensory Standard

Every scene must cover at least four senses. Show, don't tell.

```
❌ "He kissed her. She felt comfortable."

✅ "His lips carried the coolness of the iced coffee he'd just drunk.
   When his tongue touched her palate, she heard a sound from deep
   in her throat that didn't sound like her. His fingers threaded
   through the short hair at her nape, the calluses on his fingertips
   scraping across that thin skin, and her toes curled into the carpet."
```

## Quality Checklist

After every scene:
- [ ] Do the first 800 words hook the reader?
- [ ] If you remove any paragraph, would readers notice?
- [ ] At least four senses covered?
- [ ] Character desire advanced in this scene?
- [ ] Sentence length varies? (All long or all short = dead rhythm)
- [ ] Ending has a hook?

### Forbidden Patterns

- ❌ "Before they knew it" / "Somehow" (lazy transitions)
- ❌ Three+ consecutive actions without sensory description
- ❌ Adjectives instead of concrete description ("beautiful," "terrible")
- ❌ Narrator commenting on character feelings instead of showing
